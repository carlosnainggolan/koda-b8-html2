Iran War Live Updates: As Talks to End War Resume in Qatar, U.S. Conducts Strikes on Iran’s Gulf Coast

Iranian officials went to Doha on Monday for the negotiations, hours before the U.S. military announced the strikes it said were intended to protect troops. Israel’s leader said his country plans to intensify attacks against Hezbollah, Iran’s ally, in Lebanon.

Here’s the latest.

Hours after Iranian negotiators arrived in Qatar for talks on ending the war, U.S. forces struck missile launch sites in Iran and boats trying to emplace mines, American officials said Monday night. U.S. Central Command characterized the strikes in southern Iran as defensive and said they had been intended “to protect our troops from threats posed by Iranian forces.” The strikes came on a day when Israel signaled that it planned to intensify its fight against the Iranian-backed militant group Hezbollah in Lebanon.

Impact on Global Trade Routes

The escalating tension around the Strait of Hormuz has immediately triggered fresh concerns in global energy markets. This narrow waterway is a crucial transit route for the world's crude oil supply. Economic analysts warn that prolonged disruptions could lead to a significant spike in energy prices in the coming days.

Simultaneously, the Israeli Prime Minister emphasized that military operations along the northern border will continue to intensify. Defense forces have reportedly mobilized additional units to the region near Lebanon, signaling that this multi-front conflict is far from over.
